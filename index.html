About

Statistical methods for biomedical data at scale

ZengLab is led by Professor Ping Zeng at the School of Public Health, Xuzhou Medical University. The lab works at the intersection of biostatistics, statistical genetics, biomedical big data, and population health.

Our research combines modern epidemiologic designs, causal inference, machine learning, and computational statistics to study complex diseases from phenome, genome, epigenome, transcriptome, proteome, and metabolome data.

Focus Areas

Research at a glance

Multi-omics integration

Statistical models for connecting genomic, epigenomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, and metabolomic signals.

Genetic risk prediction

Polygenic prediction, cross-population transfer learning, and risk modeling for complex diseases.

Causal inference

Mendelian randomization, mediation analysis, and robust evidence triangulation for biomedical questions.

Cohort data science

Large-scale analysis of UK Biobank and other population datasets for exposure, aging, and disease progression.

Principal Investigator

Ping Zeng

Professor Zeng received his PhD in Epidemiology and Health Statistics from Nanjing Medical University, followed by postdoctoral training in the Department of Biostatistics at the University of Michigan. His group has published methodological and applied work in journals including Briefings in Bioinformatics, BMC Medicine, International Journal of Epidemiology, PLoS Computational Biology, Nature Communications, and Bioinformatics.

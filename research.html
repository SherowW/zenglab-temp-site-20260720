Research

Methods and applications for complex disease data

The lab develops statistical and computational approaches for biomedical big data, with emphasis on complex disease genetics, multi-omics integration, population cohorts, and causal discovery.

01

Multi-omics statistical modeling

We build models that integrate genetic association, eQTL, methylation, transcriptomic, proteomic, and metabolomic information to identify trait-relevant genes, tissues, pathways, and mechanisms.

02

Genetic risk prediction and transfer learning

We study polygenic prediction across ancestries and populations, including transfer-learning strategies that improve prediction for underrepresented groups by leveraging shared genetic similarity.

03

Causal inference and mediation

We develop and apply Mendelian randomization, pleiotropy-aware testing, mediation analysis, and evidence triangulation frameworks to clarify causal pathways in complex diseases.

04

Large-scale population cohort analysis

We analyze longitudinal and observational datasets to study environmental exposures, early-life factors, biological aging, cardiometabolic disease, neurodegeneration, and multimorbidity.

Infrastructure

Data and computing environment

The group works with individual-level and summary-level data resources, including large cohort, GWAS, and multi-omics datasets. Research is supported by medical statistics platforms, key laboratories, and high-performance computing resources at Xuzhou Medical University.
